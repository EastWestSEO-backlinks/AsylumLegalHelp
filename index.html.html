Comprehensive Guide to Asylum Legal Help in the United States

For individuals facing persecution in their home countries due to factors such as race, religion, nationality, political opinion, or membership in a particular social group, seeking asylum in the United States offers a pathway to protection. However, navigating the complex U.S. asylum system can be overwhelming without proper guidance and legal expertise. This is where Asylum Legal Help steps in, providing expert legal assistance to ensure that asylum seekers have the best chance of achieving safety and security.

What is Asylum in the U.S.?

Asylum is a form of protection granted to foreign nationals who are physically present in the United States and can demonstrate that they have a well-founded fear of persecution in their home country. Asylum seekers must prove that the persecution they fear is based on one of five protected grounds:

  • Race

  • Religion

  • Nationality

  • Political Opinion

  • Membership in a Particular Social Group

    • If granted asylum, individuals are allowed to remain in the U.S. and eventually apply for permanent residency (a green card), and later, U.S. citizenship.

Key Services Offered by Asylum Legal Help

  • Case Evaluation and Preparation

    • At Asylum Legal Help, we begin by evaluating your eligibility for asylum. Our team of legal experts conducts in-depth interviews to understand your background and the reasons you fear persecution. We then gather the necessary evidence, such as personal testimonies, country reports, and supporting documents, to build a strong case.

  • Filing the Application (Form I-589)

    • The asylum application process begins by filing Form I-589, the Application for Asylum and Withholding of Removal. Asylum Legal Help ensures that all sections of the form are correctly completed, that no important details are overlooked, and that the filing is done within the required one-year deadline after the applicant's arrival in the U.S.

  • Representation in Asylum Interviews

    • As part of the asylum process, applicants must attend an interview with an immigration officer at a U.S. Citizenship and Immigration Services (USCIS) office. This interview can be daunting, as it involves a detailed examination of the applicant’s background and reasons for seeking asylum. Asylum Legal Help provides full legal representation during this critical interview, ensuring that applicants are well-prepared and have a trusted legal professional by their side.

  • Defensive Asylum Representation

    • If an individual is in removal proceedings before an immigration court, they may apply for defensive asylum as a means to prevent deportation. This process is more adversarial, as the individual must prove their eligibility for asylum to a judge while facing opposition from a government attorney. Asylum Legal Help has extensive experience in courtroom proceedings and will vigorously defend the rights of individuals seeking asylum in front of an immigration judge.

  • Appeals and Legal Remedies

    • If an asylum application is denied, all is not lost. Asylum Legal Help assists clients in filing appeals with the Board of Immigration Appeals (BIA) or pursuing other legal remedies such as motions to reopen or reconsider. Our team carefully reviews the denial, identifies any errors or overlooked evidence, and works to ensure that every possible avenue for relief is explored.
